The story

Why we built this
from scratch

Most local service businesses are run by people who are exceptional at their trade — plumbing, roofing, HVAC, dental work — but who have no system for turning happy customers into public reviews. They rely on word of mouth, hope their Google profile looks decent, and lose job after job to competitors with stronger online reputations.

The tools that exist to solve this — Podium, Birdeye, NiceJob — all make the same mistake. They sell software and leave you to figure it out. A plumber running five jobs a day does not have time to manage automation campaigns, learn a CRM, or build review sequences from scratch. So the software sits unused and the reviews keep not coming.

"The difference isn't the software. It's having a person who actually manages it for you."

We built MapAuthorityPro as a done-for-you service from day one. We handle the setup, the optimization, the testing, the monthly reporting, and all seven automations that run in the background. The contractor books the job. Everything after that is us.

How we think

The philosophy behind
every decision we make

On software vs service
Tools don't get results. People do.
GoHighLevel, Podium, Birdeye — they're all just tools. What makes a reputation system actually work is someone who sets it up correctly, tests it thoroughly, monitors it monthly, and fixes it when something breaks. We are that person for every client.
On pricing
Simple pricing is a form of respect.
Hidden fees, annual contracts, and add-on pricing are ways of extracting value from people who don't have time to read the fine print. We charge a flat monthly fee that covers everything. No surprises on your second invoice.
On reviews
Timing beats everything.
The difference between a customer who leaves a review and one who doesn't is almost always timing. Ask 90 minutes after the job — when the experience is fresh and the contractor is still top of mind — and you get 3x the response rate of asking 3 days later.
On growth
One market, deeply understood.
We could have built this for restaurants, hotels, or e-commerce. We chose US service trades because we understand the workflow — job bookings, field technicians, seasonal demand, quote-based selling — and we built a system that fits that workflow exactly.
